About Me

Originally from Upstate New York along the Canadian border, I am currently a first-year graduate student in the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics at the University of Chicago. I graduated with honors from Princeton University in May 2023 with an A.B. in Astrophysics. Outside of astrophysics, I love to kayak, cook new food, and play the Wordle.

Research Interests: I am interested primarily in observational extragalactic astronomy and cosmology: galactic structure and formation, stellar populations, and near-field cosmology. I am also interested physics education research and ways to advance educational equity in astrophysics.

Research

Senior Thesis
For my senior thesis, I worked to determine the initial mass function of a set of early-type galaxies using the MASSIVE Survey spectral data and ALMA molecular gas (CO) emission data advised by Dr. Jenny Greene.

Star Formation Efficiency in Superthin Galaxies
In the summer of 2022, I was an intern in the International Internship Program at the Cardiff University Schoool of Physics and Astronomy. I worked with Dr. Timothy Davis to analyze molecular gas observations for 14 superthin galaxies, which are spiral galaxies with large axial ratios. These observations from the Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) allowed me to determine the star formation efficiency for each galaxy, which I found to be lower than typical spiral galaxies. Low star formation efficiencies support the theory of compact dark matter halos around superthin galaxies, as the added stability from a halo would increase the disk stability parameter. These results will be published in the Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society journal in late 2022/early 2023.

Spring Junior Paper: Biosignatures in exoplanet atmospheres
Advised by Dr. Adam Burrows, I conducted a literature review on the most cited biosignatures and analyzed JWST capabilities related to the detection of these gases, along with the detection of trace biosignatures such as chloroflurocarbons (CFCs).

Fall Junior Paper: X-ray variability of SgrA* during the 2018 S0-2 Star Flyby
Advised by Dr. Lena Murchikova and Dr. Neta Bahcall, I chose to expand on my USRP research to analyze the x-ray photon count fluxes and spectra of SgrA* at an unprecedented time resolution using data from the NICER mission.

Undergraduate Summer Research Program (USRP)
As a participant of USRP at Princeton University in the summer of 2021, I undertook an indepdent research project advised by Dr. Lena Murchikova. I processed data from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER) mission to measure x-ray variability at the compact source SgrA* using Heasoft (NASA). Using observations overlapping with the S0-2 star flyby in 2018, this research sought to find significant variability around the supermassive black hole with results showing flare-like behavior at Sgr* close to the flyby of S0-2. I also completed a computational Python bootcamp and attended research colloquia weekly throughout the summer.

NuMED for Children
Funded by the John C. Bogle Fellowship at Princeton University, this internship was an early undergraduate excursion into medical device engineering during the summer of 2020. I worked directly with the Head of R&D on the development of a new pediatric cardiac stent design that received FDA approval in late 2021. I contributed to improved data visualization efforts and assisted with bench testing data collection.

Outreach

Teaching: Teaching has been a major highlight of my path in astrophysics thus far. I was invited by the Department of Astrophysics at Princeton to be a teaching assistant for the introductory astronomy course AST203: The Universe. I met with students weekly during my office hours to help them with course materials, taught large-group problem sessions, and held observing sessions in using the department's telescope in Peyton Hall. Also, I have been a part-time tutor for over three years, wtih subjects including college physics, calculus, and SAT/ACT test prep. I plan to continue to pursuing teaching opportunities throughout graduate school and my career.

Outreach: Outreach is another important aspect of doing science - I am grateful for being able to share my love for astrophysics with those around me. At Princeton, I was on the Student Advisory Board for the Council of Science and Technology, where I helped plan events to increase science literacy across the campus community. I was a member of the Confernece of Undergraduate Women in Physics Local Organizing Committee and the Events Chair of the group Towards an Inclusive Community of Undergraduate Physicists (formerly Undergraduate Women in Physics).

Equity & Inclusion: As a queer woman from an underrepresented background in astrophysics, making academia and education more accesible and welcoming is critical to ensuring the field continues to have an increasing amount of diverse perspectives. My recent work with diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives involves the PhET Simulations team at the University of Colorado at Boudler. I was selected to be a member of their inaugural DEIB (diversity, equity, inclusion, and belonging) Advisory Board to help make these educational technologies accessible and representative of the diverse perspectives that can exist in physics.
